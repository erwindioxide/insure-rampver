Healthcare Terms Explained

What is Maximum Benefit Limit (MBL)?

A maximum benefit limit (MBL) refers to the consumable limit one can use per treatment/ illnesses, per person, per year.

This means you can be treated multiple times and every illness or injury is covered as long as total cost for each illness or injury is within the MBL indicated in your plan. Once you go over your MBL, extra costs will be incurred by you.

What is Annual Benefit Limit? (ABL)?

An annual benefit limit (ABL) refers to the consumable limit you can use for ALL treatments/ illnesses, per person, per year.

This means that that you can be treated multiple times and every illness or injury is covered as long as the total of all combined is within the ABL indicated in your plan. Once you go over your ABL, extra costs will be incurred by you.

What is Inpatient Care?

Inpatient care ensures that you have access to room & board, hospitalization, treatments, and medicines without extra cost.

What is Outpatient Care?

With outpatient care, get access to consultations, laboratory, and/or diagnostic exams without having to be hospitalized.

What is Preventive Care?

Preventive care is any medical service that defends against health emergencies. It includes doctor visits, such as annual physicals, well-woman appointments, and dental cleanings. Some medicines are preventive, such as immunizations, contraception, and allergy medications.

What are Pre-Existing Conditions?

Any sickness, illness, disease, injury, disorder, or ailment that you have even before applying for a healthcare plan is considered a pre existing condition?

What are Dreaded Diseases?

These are conditions or illnesses that are considered to be chronic, progressive, life-threatening, and/or may entail lifelong therapy. It also refers to conditions wherein complete cure may not be ensured. Examples are Cancer, Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease, Lupus, and arthritis.

What is Travel Assist?

In case you require medical services outside of the Philippines, HMOs may be able to assist you. Note that travel assist means that the medical services you will get is not covered or will not be reimbursed (unless explicitly indicated in your HMO plan). It only means that your HMO provider can assist you through telephone medical assistance, medical service provider referral, appointment arrangement with local doctors for treatment, and arrangement of hospital admission.
